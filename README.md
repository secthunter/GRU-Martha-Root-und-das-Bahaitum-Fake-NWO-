## Overview

The project compiles and analyzes information from various sources to explore potential links between the Bahá'í Faith, geopolitical intelligence operations, and global conspiracy narratives. Key elements include:

## Research Focus

### Bahá'í Faith Background
The Bahá'í Faith is a monotheistic religion founded by Bahá'u'lláh in the 19th century, emphasizing the essential worth of all religions, unity of humanity, and progressive revelation. It originated in Persia (modern Iran) and has spread globally.

### Martha Root's Role
Martha Louise Root (1872-1939) was a prominent American Bahá'í teacher who traveled extensively, meeting heads of state and promoting Bahá'í principles. Her 1921 trip to Russia, where she presented Bahá'í teachings to Lenin and Trotsky, forms a key point of conspiracy theories linking the faith to Soviet influence.

### Conspiracy Theory Investigation
The repository investigates claims that:
- The Bahá'í Faith is a GRU (Russian military intelligence) operation disguised as a religion.
- Martha Root served as an intelligence asset during her travels.
- The faith's teachings on world unity are part of a "Fake NWO" plot for global control.

## Findings Summary
- Bahá'í Faith: Legitimate religious movement with documented history; no evidence of AI derivation.
- Martha Root: Dedicated teacher; her Soviet visit was for religious promotion, not espionage.
- GRU/NWO Links: No substantiated evidence of intelligence connections; appears to be fringe conspiracy theories.
- URLs: Benign research links; no exploitation vectors identified.
- Deepened Research (Browser Exploration): Bahá'í Faith has historically faced accusations of espionage for various nations (Russia, Britain, US, Israel) in Iran due to political propaganda. Martha Root met Lenin and Trotsky in 1921 to discuss Bahá'í teachings. No direct GRU involvement found, but Soviet GRU predecessor may be referenced in historical accusations.
